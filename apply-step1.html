Step 1 – Apply for a NAISS/SUPR Account

To request compute and storage resources, you must first have an active user account in the NAISS/SUPR system.

What is NAISS/SUPR?

NAISS (National Academic Infrastructure for Supercomputing in Sweden) provides national high-performance computing and storage resources.
SUPR is the web portal used to manage:

  • user accounts
  • project applications
  • resource allocations

You access SUPR here: https://supr.naiss.se

Who Can Apply?

  • Employees and researchers at SLU

Create a SUPR Account

  1. Fill out & submit account request form.
Tip
  • Use Register with Federated Identity if you are enrolled in a Swedish university.
  • If you are not enrolled in a Swedish university or if the previous option doesn’t work for you, use instead Register without Federated Identity.
  1. Fill in your contact details and affiliation with SLU.
  2. Confirm your email address if requested.
Tip

When registering or updating your profile:

  • Select SLU as your organisation.
  • Add your research group, department or unit in the profile description if relevant.

This makes it easier to link you to the correct projects and allocations.

Accept NAISS user agreement

Just after creating the SUPR account, on the page you are taken to, there will be a section titled “User Agreement” at the very top. Just under it is the button named “Accept NAISS User Agreement”.

Alternately, if you do this at a later point. Login to SUPR. At the very top it will say “NAISS User Agreement Pending”.

Tip
  • Use With SWAMID if you are enrolled in a Swedish university - this option is much faster!
  • If you are not enrolled in a Swedish university use instead Alternative: Offline via Paper Form. Here you will have to print out, sign and send in the form. This may take up to a week.

After Your Account Is Created

Once your SUPR account is active, you can:

  • be added as a member to existing projects, and
  • submit new applications in calls such as “SLU Local 2025”.
